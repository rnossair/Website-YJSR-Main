How does DNA work?

Posted by Rohini Vummadi on July 17, 2022

Introduction

We all learned in biology class that DNA stands for deoxyribonucleic acid and that its structure is a double helix. But there is more to it.

Whatâ€™s the function of DNA?

It is a molecule that codes the genetic instructions that inform living creatures how to develop physically, live, and reproduce.

Where is DNA found?

As we all know, DNA is found inside every cell and is passed down from the parents to the offspring.

What does DNA comprise of?

DNA is comprised of molecules known as nucleotides. Each nucleotide contains 3 components: a phosphate group, a nitrogen base, and a sugar molecule. There are 4 types of nitrogen bases: A (adenine), T (thymine), G (guanine), and C (cytosine). Combined together, they build the genetic code of your DNA. In the double helix structure, A pairs with T, & G with C.

Fun facts!

  1. According to the US National Library of Medicine, the human DNA is comprised of around 3 billion base pairs and more than 99% the bases are the same in all people!
  2. There are about 3 million DNA bases in our genome!
  3. We share 9% percent of DNA with other humans!
  4. As evolution progressed, humans have lost 500+ DNA codes.
  5. We share about 60% of genes with fruit flies!
  6. A new technology called CRISPR can allow us to edit genomes, which can be led to a cure of cancer, any disease with a genetic component!
  7. We share 41% of DNA with a banana, 85% with a mouse, and 40% with a fruitfly!

Conclusion

This is the basic information regarding DNA. There is obviously much more to acquire, in terms of details- but that is for a later article.
